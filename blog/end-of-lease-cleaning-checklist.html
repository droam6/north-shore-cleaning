End of Lease Cleaning Checklist for Sydney Renters

Moving out of a rental property is stressful enough without the added worry of whether you will get your bond back. In New South Wales, your landlord or property manager can make a claim against your bond if the property is not returned in a reasonably clean condition, allowing for fair wear and tear. The key phrase is "reasonably clean" -- and what seems clean to you may not meet the standards of a fussy property manager conducting a final inspection.

Our team has performed hundreds of end of lease cleans across Sydney's North Shore, from apartments in North Sydney and Neutral Bay to family homes in Chatswood and Killara. This comprehensive checklist covers everything you need to address to pass your final inspection and get your full bond back.

What Is End of Lease Cleaning?

End of lease cleaning, also called bond cleaning or vacate cleaning, is a thorough deep clean of the entire rental property performed before you hand back the keys. It goes far beyond regular cleaning and aims to return the property to the condition it was in at the start of your tenancy, accounting for reasonable wear and tear.

Under NSW tenancy law, tenants are required to leave the property in a "reasonably clean" condition. While the legislation does not define a specific cleaning standard, most property managers and landlords expect the property to be cleaned to a professional standard. Your original condition report is the benchmark -- any cleaning issues noted at the start of your tenancy cannot be held against you at the end.

Most rental bonds on the North Shore range from four to six weeks' rent, which for the area can easily amount to $2,000 to $5,000 or more. A professional end of lease clean typically costs a fraction of this amount and provides peace of mind that the job is done properly.

Complete Room-by-Room Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure you cover every area that property managers inspect. We have organised it room by room to make it easy to work through systematically.

Kitchen

The kitchen is the area most likely to cause bond disputes. Property managers inspect kitchens closely because grease, grime and food residue build up over the course of a tenancy.

  • Clean the oven thoroughly inside and out, including racks, trays, door glass (between the panels if accessible) and the grill element
  • Clean the rangehood, including filters, exterior surfaces and the vent
  • Clean the stovetop and all burners, removing built-up grease and food residue
  • Wipe down all cabinet doors, handles and the tops of cabinets
  • Clean inside all cabinets and drawers, removing crumbs and liners
  • Clean the dishwasher interior, filter, door seal and exterior
  • Clean the fridge inside and out, including shelves, drawers, seals and the top of the unit
  • Clean the microwave inside and out
  • Scrub the sink and polish taps, removing any limescale
  • Clean the splashback tiles and grout
  • Wipe down benchtops and clean any stains
  • Clean light fixtures and switches
  • Mop the floor, paying attention to corners, under the fridge and along skirting boards

Bathroom and Ensuite

Bathrooms are the second most scrutinised area during inspections. Mould, soap scum and limescale are the main concerns.

  • Scrub all tile surfaces and grout, removing mould and soap scum
  • Clean the shower recess thoroughly, including screen, tracks, taps and showerhead
  • Descale taps and showerheads
  • Clean the bathtub inside and around the edges, including any caulking
  • Clean the toilet thoroughly: bowl, seat, lid, base, behind the cistern and the floor around it
  • Clean the vanity, basin and mirror
  • Wipe down cabinets inside and out
  • Clean exhaust fan covers (remove and wash if possible)
  • Clean towel rails, hooks and any accessories
  • Remove any mould from silicone seals around the bath or shower
  • Mop the floor, including behind the toilet and in corners

Bedrooms

  • Vacuum or mop all floors, including inside wardrobes and along skirting boards
  • Clean inside all built-in wardrobes: shelves, hanging rails, drawers and mirrors
  • Wipe down all surfaces including window sills, skirting boards and architraves
  • Clean light fixtures, switches and power points
  • Clean windows inside and out (if accessible), including tracks and frames
  • Clean curtain tracks or blinds (dust venetians, wipe roller blinds)
  • Remove any marks or scuffs from walls (use a melamine sponge for most marks)
  • Clean door handles and door surfaces

Living Areas

  • Vacuum or mop all floors, moving into corners and along edges
  • Clean skirting boards and architraves
  • Dust and wipe down all surfaces, shelving and built-in units
  • Clean light fixtures, ceiling fans and air conditioning vents
  • Clean all windows inside and out, including tracks, frames and sills
  • Clean blinds or curtain tracks
  • Remove marks from walls
  • Clean fireplace (if applicable), including hearth and surround
  • Clean sliding door tracks and glass

Laundry

  • Clean inside and behind the washing machine space (if machine removed)
  • Clean the laundry tub and taps
  • Wipe down cabinets inside and out
  • Clean the dryer vent and lint filter area
  • Mop the floor, including behind appliances and in corners
  • Clean any wall-mounted drying racks or hooks

Outdoor Areas

  • Sweep balconies, patios, courtyards and carports
  • Remove cobwebs from exterior walls, eaves and outdoor lights
  • Clean outdoor light fixtures
  • Wipe down balcony railings
  • Clean the front door and entrance area
  • Sweep or blow the garage floor (if applicable)
  • Ensure gardens are tidy and lawns are mowed (if your responsibility under the lease)

Common Mistakes That Cost Your Bond

After performing hundreds of end of lease cleans, we have seen the same mistakes come up repeatedly. Avoiding these common oversights can be the difference between getting your full bond back and facing a dispute.

  • Forgetting the oven: This is the single most common reason for bond deductions. A quick wipe of the oven door is not enough. Property managers will open the oven and inspect inside, including the back wall, roof, element and the glass door panels. Use a proper oven cleaner and allow adequate soaking time.
  • Ignoring window tracks: Dirty window tracks are a red flag for property managers because they suggest the property has not been thoroughly cleaned. Use a stiff brush, vacuum the debris, then wipe with a damp cloth.
  • Leaving marks on walls: Scuffs from furniture, marks from sticky tape, picture hook holes and general marks are all things you need to address. Use a melamine sponge (magic eraser) for most marks and fill small holes with wall filler, sanding smooth once dry.
  • Overlooking exhaust fans: Dusty bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans are easy to miss but always noticed during inspections. Remove the covers and wash them in warm soapy water.
  • Skipping the tops of doors and cabinets: The tops of doors, door frames and the area above kitchen cabinets accumulate significant dust and grease. Wipe all elevated surfaces.
  • Neglecting light fixtures: Remove light shades and wash them to remove dust and dead insects. Wipe down fixed fittings with a damp cloth.
